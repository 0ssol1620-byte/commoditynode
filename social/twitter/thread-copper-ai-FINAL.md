**[댓글 1]**

Let's talk numbers.

A single hyperscale data center uses 20,000-40,000 tonnes of copper — wiring, busbars, cooling systems, transformers.

Microsoft alone plans 50+ new facilities by 2028.
Google: 30+.
Amazon: 40+.

That's ~2M tonnes of NEW copper demand. Annual global production? 22M tonnes.

---

**[댓글 2]**

Now add the supply crisis nobody's talking about.

Average time to permit a new copper mine: 16.5 years.
Chile's output dropped 5.4% YoY — their aging mines are depleting.
Peru (world #2) had 47 days of mining protests in 2025 alone.

There is NO supply response coming for years.

---

**[댓글 3]**

Here's where geopolitics gets spicy. 🌶️

Iran-Israel tensions → Hormuz Strait risk → oil premium → energy costs for smelters spike.

Copper smelting is ENERGY-INTENSIVE. A 15% rise in energy costs = $0.20/lb added to copper production costs.

The floor price is rising whether you like it or not.

---

**[댓글 4]**

The US-China tariff war is the real bomb.

Trump's 145% tariffs on Chinese goods? China retaliated with rare earth + refined metals export controls.

China processes 40% of the world's copper concentrate. If they restrict exports of refined copper, the West has maybe 6-8 months of inventory.

---

**[댓글 5]**

"But copper has substitutes!"

No. It doesn't. Not for electrical conductivity at scale.

Aluminum is 61% as conductive and corrodes at copper joints. Fiber optics can't carry power. Silver works but costs 80x more.

For AI infrastructure, copper is literally irreplaceable.
