Crude oil is at $100.98 today. Our AI model (Amazon Chronos-2) is forecasting $92.13 by July — an 8.8% decline.

Here's the structural case, and why I think the uncertainty is actually the story.

**The bearish median:**

The model's base case reflects three converging forces:
- US-China tariff escalation (145%) compressing trade volumes and derived fuel demand
- US shale production at near-record levels — break-even well below current prices
- OPEC+ cohesion eroding: Iraq and Kazakhstan consistently producing above quota

**The extreme upside tail ($138 in the 80% band):**

The model isn't being reckless with that upper bound. The Strait of Hormuz handles 21% of global seaborne oil daily. A serious disruption — which IRGC live-fire exercises near shipping lanes make non-trivial — pushes Brent above $120 within 30 days by most scenarios. This is a genuine low-probability, high-impact risk that deserves explicit weighting.

**What I find most interesting for portfolio construction:**

The $71 range between bear ($66.80) and bull ($138.08) case is essentially telling you to be long optionality here, not direction. Long crude vol, or positions that benefit from a move in either direction, seem more appropriate than a strong directional bet.

**Near-term catalyst: April 7 OPEC+ Committee Meeting**

Saudi Arabia's production stance will determine whether the bearish trend accelerates or reverses. A unilateral cut announcement would immediately invalidate the bearish median forecast.

**Equity read-through:**
Airlines (DAL, UAL), logistics (UPS), and chemical manufacturers benefit from lower crude. Energy producers (XOM, CVX) have downside leverage but geopolitical premium as partial offset.
