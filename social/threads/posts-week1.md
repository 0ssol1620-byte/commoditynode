**Post 1: Copper & AI**

Everyone's trading NVDA for the AI boom. Meanwhile, nobody's talking about copper — the metal that physically makes data centers work.

Each data center needs 20,000–40,000 tonnes of copper. 300+ are being built. Annual supply: 22M tonnes. New mines take 16 years.

The AI trade isn't just chips. It's copper, concrete, and power. The physical layer always gets its moment.

**Post 2: Gold**

Gold at $4,500 and everyone's calling it a top.

Here's what they're missing: central banks bought 1,045 tonnes last year. China's gold is still only 5% of reserves. After Russia's $300B got frozen, every country learned the same lesson — gold can't be sanctioned.

This isn't retail FOMO. It's sovereign insurance. Different game.

**Post 3: Wheat**

The scariest chart in commodities right now is wheat stocks-to-use at 27%. Lowest since the 2007/08 food crisis.

Ukraine production still crippled. Russia in a drought. Australia dry. EU yields down. And the market is net short.

800 million people depend on Black Sea wheat. Nobody's paying attention. That's usually when things get interesting.

**Post 4: Lithium**

Lithium crashed 92%. From $80K to $6K per tonne.

The paradox? EV sales are at all-time highs. Battery demand growing 25% annually.

At $6K, 40% of mines lose money. They're shutting down. Same mines that take 3–5 years to restart.

Commodity markets are brutal: today's bust is planting the seeds of the next boom. Seen this movie before with uranium. Same ending.

**Post 5: Uranium**

The quietest bull case in all of commodities: uranium.

Demand: 180M lbs/year. Supply: 140M lbs. Deficit: 40M lbs — filled by stockpiles that are running out.

40 new reactors being built. 15 years of zero mine investment. Kazakhstan (45% of supply) can't hit targets.

EU says nuclear is green. US passed bipartisan support. China building 24 reactors.

The supply crisis is here. It's just quiet. For now.
