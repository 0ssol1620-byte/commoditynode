The Gold-Silver ratio just hit 64:1.

Historical average: 67:1
Below 65 historically: Silver outperforms Gold by +12% avg over 60 days
Win rate: 71%

This is one of the most reliable cross-commodity signals in the market.

Why it works: gold and silver share the same macro drivers (dollar, rates, risk sentiment). But silver has additional industrial demand from solar panels, EVs, and electronics. When the ratio compresses, it often signals silver catching up to fair value.

Current read: Silver at $74.59 is undervalued relative to gold at $4,712.

## POST 2 (490자)

The Copper-Gold ratio is near a 3-year low.

This ratio is sometimes called the "economic barometer."

When it's rising: markets expect growth, copper (industrial) outperforms
When it's falling: growth concerns dominate, gold (safe haven) leads

At current levels, historical data shows copper has averaged +15% over the following 90 days when the ratio is this depressed.

Win rate on this setup: 68%

This could mean either copper bounces hard, or gold corrects — or both. The ratio has to normalize somewhere.
