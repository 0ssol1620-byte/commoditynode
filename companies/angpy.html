Company Overview

📈 Platinum Futures Price (PL=F) — ANGPY's primary revenue driver

Anglo American Platinum (Amplats) is the world's largest primary platinum producer, operating vast UG2 and Merensky reef mines in South Africa's Bushveld Complex. As a subsidiary of Anglo American PLC, Amplats commands approximately 40% of global primary platinum supply through its Mogalakwena, Unki, and joint-venture operations. The company is undergoing a strategic restructuring to focus on higher-grade, palladium-rich ore bodies while rationalizing labor-intensive conventional mining operations. Amplats is the benchmark platinum equity globally.

Commodity Exposures

Platinum Palladium Rhodium

Platinum is the foundation of Amplats' revenue base — the company's Mogalakwena open-pit mine, the world's largest open-cut PGM mine, produces a palladium-rich ore body that also yields platinum and rhodium. Palladium provides significant volume-weighted earnings, while rhodium — the highest-value PGM per kilogram — generates outsized revenue per ounce despite smaller production volumes. The PGM basket price (weighted by volumes of Pt, Pd, Rh, Ru, Ir) is the key composite metric for Amplats' earnings.

Price Sensitivity

Platinum price movements form the largest single earnings driver for Amplats given its production volumes and the metal's relatively stable industrial demand base (automotive catalysts, jewelry, hydrogen fuel cells). Palladium provides volume-weighted leverage, while rhodium is the highest-value-per-kg product and the most volatile — rhodium's explosive moves (from $600/oz to $29,000/oz in 2021 and back) can be a major swing factor in annual earnings. South African operational risks — Eskom power reliability, labor relations at shaft-mining operations, and rand currency swings — add significant equity volatility beyond the PGM price cycle.
