Company Overview

Tyson Foods processes approximately 20% of US beef, 17% of chicken, and 18% of pork. Branded products command premium pricing and reduce direct commodity exposure. However, the company remains highly sensitive to cattle, hog, and grain prices as its largest input costs. Restructuring initiatives targeting $1B in cost savings have aimed to improve margin resilience through commodity cycles. Chicken operations provide somewhat more predictable margins as Tyson raises its own flocks versus purchasing cattle and hogs from independent producers.

Price Sensitivity

A $10/cwt change in live cattle prices impacts annual processing costs by approximately $500M. Corn and soybean meal are primary feed inputs for chicken operations. The company typically hedges 30-60% of grain exposure 6-18 months forward. Chicken margins are most controllable due to vertical integration; beef and pork are more exposed to spot cattle/hog pricing. The current US cattle supply shortage is extending packer margin compression through at least 2027.
