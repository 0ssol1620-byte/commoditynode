Company Overview

📈 MP Materials (MP) — direct rare earth equity proxy

MP Materials is the owner and operator of Mountain Pass, the only rare earth mine and processing facility in the Western Hemisphere, located in the Mojave Desert of California. The company produces rare earth concentrate used in permanent magnets for EV motors, wind turbines, defense systems, and consumer electronics. MP Materials produces approximately 15% of global rare earth supply and is building out the only integrated Western rare earth supply chain — from mining through magnet alloy production — with US government strategic partnerships supporting the effort.

Commodity Exposures

Rare Earth Elements

Neodymium-praseodymium (NdPr) oxide is the most commercially important rare earth product from Mountain Pass and the primary value driver for MP Materials. NdPr is the key ingredient in neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) permanent magnets used in EV traction motors and wind turbine generators. The company has historically sold rare earth concentrate to China for separation and processing, but is building domestic separation and downstream magnet alloy capabilities to capture more value. Lanthanum and cerium (light rare earths) are produced in large volumes but have limited commercial value.

Price Sensitivity

NdPr oxide price is the critical earnings driver — it exhibits high correlation to EV production rates and wind energy buildout. China's dominance in rare earth separation and magnet production creates significant pricing power for Chinese competitors, which can pressure NdPr prices through strategic oversupply. Each 10% change in NdPr prices materially impacts MP's revenue per tonne sold. The company's US government strategic partnerships — including Department of Defense contracts — provide a partially subsidized demand floor and support for its domestic processing expansion. Geopolitical risk (China export restrictions on rare earths) could dramatically increase NdPr prices.
