Company Overview

American multinational that designs and manufactures semiconductor chips, historically dominating the x86 processor market for PCs and data centers.

Commodity Exposures

Copper Gold Natural Gas

Price Sensitivity

Intel's semiconductor fabs consume enormous quantities of energy, water, and specialty materials. The company's push to advanced process nodes (Intel 18A, Intel 20A) requires increasingly pure materials and more energy-intensive manufacturing. Intel's new fab construction in Ohio and Germany creates significant steel, copper, and concrete demand. Each fab costs $20-30 billion and takes years to build.
