Company Overview

📈 Cocoa Futures Price (CC=F) — MDLZ's most critical input commodity

Mondelez International is a global snack food giant generating approximately $35 billion in annual revenue across iconic brands: Oreo, Cadbury, Toblerone, Milka, Chips Ahoy, Triscuit, and Ritz. The company is among the world's largest buyers of cocoa beans, purchasing approximately 15% of global supply annually for its chocolate-heavy product portfolio. Mondelez was spun off from Kraft Foods in 2012 specifically to focus on higher-growth snacking and confectionery categories. Chocolate represents the largest and most cocoa-sensitive product segment.

Commodity Exposures

Cocoa Sugar Wheat

Cocoa is the defining commodity for Mondelez's profitability. When cocoa prices spike — as they did dramatically in 2024 when prices exceeded $10,000/tonne due to West African crop failures — margins compress rapidly across the chocolate portfolio. Sugar is the second-largest commodity input, relevant for both confectionery and biscuit categories. Wheat matters for the biscuit (Oreo, Ritz, Chips Ahoy) and cracker segments. Dairy ingredients (milk powder, butter) are also significant but less volatile than cocoa. The company's pricing power in premium chocolate (Cadbury, Toblerone, Milka) is real but constrained by private-label substitution when price gaps widen too far.

Price Sensitivity

A 10% increase in cocoa prices creates approximately $300 million in gross profit headwind for Mondelez. Pricing recovery typically takes 6-9 months to flow through as price increases require customer negotiation and retailer acceptance. The company's hedging program typically covers 12-18 months of cocoa needs, creating a lag between spot price moves and P&L impact. Sustained cocoa price inflation above $8,000/tonne tests the company's ability to pass through costs without losing volume to lower-priced alternatives. Sugar price increases compound the challenge when they occur simultaneously with cocoa spikes, as both affect chocolate margins.
