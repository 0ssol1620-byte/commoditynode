Company Overview

JBS is the world's largest protein company by revenue, processing beef, chicken, pork, and lamb across four continents. In the US, JBS operates through its ~80% stake in Pilgrim's Pride (PPC), the second-largest US chicken producer. JBS USA is the largest US beef processor. Brazilian operations process beef for domestic consumption and export to 150+ countries. Geographic and protein diversification provides natural hedging against single-market protein cycles — when US beef margins compress, Brazilian exports or global chicken demand may offset.

Price Sensitivity

JBS earnings are highly sensitive to protein price cycles across multiple species simultaneously. US beef packing margins can swing $200-400/head within a quarter depending on cattle availability. Chicken operations at Pilgrim's Pride are exposed to corn and soybean meal feed costs. Brazil's real/dollar exchange rate is a secondary driver — a weaker real boosts Brazilian earnings in dollar terms. JBS scale provides procurement and logistics advantages but also creates concentration risk in disease events.
