Company Overview

📈 Silver Futures Price (SI=F) — PAAS's primary revenue driver

Pan American Silver is one of the world's largest silver producers, operating mines across Latin America in Peru, Mexico, Bolivia, Argentina, and Chile. The company's acquisition of Tahoe Resources in 2019 added the Escobal silver mine in Guatemala and the Shahuindo/La Arena gold mines in Peru. The transformative 2023 acquisition of Yamana Gold's Latin American assets — including the Jacobina and El Peñón mines — made Pan American a significant gold producer as well, with gold now representing approximately 50% of revenue. Annual production runs roughly 42 million silver equivalent ounces.

Commodity Exposures

Silver Gold

Silver is Pan American's identity and largest production commodity, with the company's most significant silver operations in Peru (Huaron, Morococha) and Mexico (La Colorada, Dolores). The Yamana acquisition significantly increased gold's share of revenue, creating a more balanced precious metals portfolio. Silver and gold prices historically move together but silver is more volatile and provides higher upside leverage. By-product zinc, lead, and copper from polymetallic silver mines reduce net production costs and contribute to overall profitability.

Price Sensitivity

PAAS exhibits 2x-3x leverage to physical silver prices versus simply holding silver, due to operating leverage in mining — fixed costs are relatively constant, so silver price increases flow disproportionately to earnings. Each $1/oz change in silver price impacts annual revenue by approximately $40-50 million. Gold provides a stability anchor: when silver underperforms, gold assets (now ~50% of revenue) maintain earnings power. Geographic diversification across 7 countries reduces single-country risk but adds political and permitting complexity, particularly in Latin American jurisdictions.
