Company Overview

Glencore is uniquely structured as both the world's largest commodity trading house and a major diversified mining company. Its trading arm handles over 3 million tonnes of copper-equivalent commodity flows daily, providing market intelligence and margin that pure miners lack. Mining operations span copper (DRC, Peru, Australia), cobalt (~25% of global supply), nickel, thermal and metallurgical coal, and zinc. The cobalt position makes Glencore the most direct large-cap exposure to EV battery supply chains among diversified miners.

Commodity Exposures

Copper Cobalt Nickel Zinc Coal

Copper is the single largest mining earnings driver, but cobalt is Glencore's most distinctive exposure — its Katanga and Mutanda mines in DRC produce roughly 25% of global cobalt supply. Coal trading provides substantial cash flow but ESG pressure remains a valuation overhang. The trading division's unique market intelligence allows Glencore to profit from commodity price dislocations that hurt pure producers.

Price Sensitivity

Glencore's diversified model means no single commodity dominates earnings as severely as in pure-plays. A 10% copper move impacts EBITDA by ~$1.5B. Cobalt sensitivity is higher on a percentage basis — each $5,000/tonne cobalt move impacts ~$400M EBITDA. The trading division typically contributes $2-3B of adjusted EBIT annually, acting as a natural hedge during commodity downturns when the trading book benefits from volatility and dislocation.
