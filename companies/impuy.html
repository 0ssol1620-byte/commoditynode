Company Overview

Impala Platinum operates its flagship Impala Rustenburg and Marula mines in South Africa's Bushveld Complex, plus Impala Canada (formerly North American Palladium). The company produces approximately 14% of global palladium and is a significant platinum and rhodium producer. The Waterberg palladium-platinum project in South Africa's Northern Limb represents long-term growth optionality. South Africa's energy and logistics challenges create ongoing operational risk that is reflected in a structural discount versus assets in stable jurisdictions.

Price Sensitivity

Similar leverage structure to SBSW. Each $100/oz palladium move impacts EBITDA by ~$150M. Rhodium production of ~30,000 oz/year creates meaningful earnings leverage — the $29,000/oz 2021 peak drove extraordinary profits while the subsequent crash compressed margins severely. Platinum provides stable base revenue. Rand/dollar exchange rate is a key secondary driver since costs are rand-denominated while revenue is dollar-priced.
