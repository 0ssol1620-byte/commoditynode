Company Overview

📈 Platinum Futures Price (PL=F) — IMPUY's primary revenue driver

Impala Platinum is South Africa's second-largest PGM producer, operating the Impala Rustenburg mine complex and Marula mine in the Bushveld Complex, plus international operations through Impala Canada (formerly North American Palladium). The company produces approximately 14% of global palladium supply alongside significant platinum and rhodium output. The Waterberg project in Limpopo province represents a major future growth asset with potential to become a large-scale, mechanized PGM operation.

Commodity Exposures

Platinum Palladium Rhodium

Impala Platinum's revenue is driven by a basket of PGMs — platinum, palladium, and rhodium — with the mix depending on which reef (UG2 vs Merensky) is being mined. Palladium and rhodium typically command higher per-ounce prices than platinum, making the revenue split more balanced than production volumes might suggest. Rhodium exposure is particularly meaningful at elevated price levels, as even small production volumes translate into substantial revenue when rhodium trades above $5,000/oz.

Price Sensitivity

IMPUY exhibits similar leverage characteristics to Sibanye Stillwater (SBSW) as a South African PGM producer. The company's sensitivity to PGM basket prices is amplified by relatively high fixed costs in deep-level mining. Rhodium price movements have an outsized impact on profitability due to the metal's extreme price volatility. South African operational risks — including Eskom power supply, labor relations, and Transnet rail logistics — add a country-risk premium to the equity. The Impala Canada operations provide some geographic diversification but represent a smaller share of overall production.
