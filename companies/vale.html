Company Overview

📈 Copper Futures Price (HG=F) — proxy for Vale's base metals exposure

Vale is the world's largest iron ore producer, operating the massive Carajás complex in Brazil's Pará state — producing approximately 320 million tonnes per year of high-grade iron ore. The company's S11D mine at Carajás is the world's largest iron ore operation, producing premium 65% Fe content ore that commands a quality premium over competitors. Vale is also the world's second-largest nickel producer, with operations in Canada (Sudbury, Thompson, Voisey's Bay), Indonesia (PTVI), and New Caledonia, positioning it as a key player in the EV battery supply chain.

Commodity Exposures

Iron Ore Nickel Copper

Iron ore accounts for approximately 75-80% of Vale's revenue, making it overwhelmingly the company's primary commodity exposure. The Carajás premium (high-Fe content) means Vale benefits disproportionately when Chinese steel mills prioritize ore quality to reduce emissions. Nickel operations represent the company's energy transition bet, with Class 1 nickel suitable for EV battery cathodes. Copper production from Salobo and Sossego mines in Brazil provides additional base metals diversification.

Price Sensitivity

Vale's earnings are dominated by iron ore price movements. Each $10/tonne change in iron ore price translates to approximately $2 billion in EBITDA impact — making Vale one of the most iron-ore-leveraged equities globally. The company's low-cost Carajás operations (C1 cash cost ~$18-20/tonne) mean it remains profitable even at depressed iron ore prices, but the high fixed-cost nature of mining creates substantial operating leverage on the upside. Nickel provides upside optionality as EV adoption accelerates, with Vale's Class 1 nickel production positioned for battery-grade demand.
