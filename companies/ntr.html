Company Overview

📈 Natural Gas Futures Price (NG=F) — key input cost for nitrogen fertilizer production

Nutrien is the world's largest producer of potash and a top-3 nitrogen fertilizer producer, operating 28 production facilities globally. Formed from the 2018 merger of PotashCorp and Agrium, the company combines massive upstream fertilizer production with the world's largest agricultural retail network — over 1,700 stores across North America serving farmers with crop inputs, services, and digital agriculture solutions. Nutrien controls approximately 20% of global potash production capacity from its Saskatchewan mines.

Commodity Exposures

Natural Gas Potash Phosphate Ammonia

Natural gas is Nutrien's most important input cost — it is the primary feedstock for nitrogen fertilizer production (ammonia, urea, UAN). Higher natural gas prices raise Nutrien's production costs but can also benefit the company by pushing higher-cost competitors out of the market. Potash pricing operates independently of natural gas, driven instead by global food security demand, Chinese/Indian contract negotiations, and supply disruptions (e.g., Belarus sanctions). Phosphate and ammonia provide additional fertilizer segment revenue.

Price Sensitivity

Natural gas price is the key cost driver for Nutrien's nitrogen segment — each $1/MMBtu change in North American gas prices impacts nitrogen production costs significantly. However, Nutrien benefits from North America's relatively cheap gas vs. European/Asian competitors, providing a structural cost advantage. Potash prices are driven by global food demand and are independent of gas prices — geopolitical disruptions (Belarus sanctions, Russia/Ukraine conflict) can cause dramatic price spikes. The retail agricultural network provides a more stable earnings base that partially insulates the company from fertilizer price volatility.
