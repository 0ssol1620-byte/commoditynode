Company Overview

Nutrien controls approximately 20% of global potash capacity from its Saskatchewan mines, positioning it as the price-setter in the world's most concentrated commodity market. The nitrogen business is natural gas-intensive — Nutrien produces ~10M tonnes of nitrogen fertilizer annually, with natural gas representing 60-70% of production costs. The retail network of 1,700+ agricultural centers provides downstream distribution advantage, capturing margin from precision agriculture services and crop protection products. Retail reduces earnings volatility versus pure production peers.

Commodity Exposures

Natural Gas Potash Phosphate Ammonia

Price Sensitivity

Natural gas is the critical cost driver for nitrogen production — a $1/MMBtu change in gas price impacts nitrogen EBITDA by approximately $300M annually. Potash pricing is driven by global food demand and Chinese/Indian import contracts that set seasonal benchmarks. A $50/tonne potash price move impacts EBITDA by approximately $700M. The retail segment provides more stable earnings (~$500-600M EBITDA through cycles) that partially offsets fertilizer price volatility.
