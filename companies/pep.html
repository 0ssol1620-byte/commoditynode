Company Overview

📈 Corn Futures Price (ZC=F) — PepsiCo's largest single commodity exposure

PepsiCo is a global food and beverage giant generating approximately $90 billion in annual revenue. The company's portfolio spans Frito-Lay (corn-based snacks: Doritos, Lay's, Cheetos), Quaker Foods (oats, cereals, Rice-A-Roni), Pepsi beverages, Gatorade, and Tropicana OJ. Corn is the single largest commodity input across multiple divisions — as a base for corn chips, high-fructose corn syrup (HFCS) for beverages, and corn-based sweeteners. The Quaker division makes PepsiCo one of the largest direct oat buyers globally.

Commodity Exposures

Corn Oats Sugar Wheat

Corn dominates PepsiCo's commodity cost base as the primary input for Frito-Lay snacks and HFCS sweeteners for beverages. Oats are a significant secondary exposure through the Quaker brand — PepsiCo buys millions of bushels annually for oatmeal, granola bars, and cereals. Sugar is important for international beverage markets where cane sugar is used instead of HFCS. Wheat costs affect Quaker grain products and select snack categories. Natural gas is a significant manufacturing input across PepsiCo's global production network.

Price Sensitivity

A 10% change in corn prices translates to approximately $200 million in annual earnings impact for PepsiCo. The company typically hedges commodity costs 6-18 months forward, smoothing price volatility but creating a lag between market moves and P&L impact. Sustained commodity inflation eventually pressures margins unless offset by pricing — PepsiCo has demonstrated strong pricing power across its portfolio but faces limits when consumers trade down to private label. The branded premium (Lay's vs. store brand chips) provides buffer but erodes during prolonged inflationary periods.
