Company Overview

PepsiCo is one of the world's most diversified food and beverage companies. Corn is the single largest commodity exposure — it is the primary ingredient in Frito-Lay chips, provides HFCS for beverages, and feeds animals in ingredient supply chains. The Quaker division makes PepsiCo a direct participant in oat markets — a growing role as oat milk demand expands. Natural gas is significant for manufacturing operations globally. PepsiCo's scale and brand portfolio allow partial pass-through of commodity cost inflation to consumers.

Price Sensitivity

Corn price changes impact PepsiCo through direct ingredient costs and indirect supply chain costs — estimated $200M annual EBITDA impact per 10% corn price move. The commodity hedging program typically covers 6-18 months of key inputs forward, smoothing quarterly earnings. Pricing actions generally lag commodity cost increases by 2-4 quarters but historically recover full margin within 12-18 months. Natural gas is significant for manufacturing facilities globally.
