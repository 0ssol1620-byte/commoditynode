Company Overview

Southern Copper operates Cuajone and Toquepala in Peru and the massive Buenavista del Cobre mine in Mexico. As a Grupo Mexico subsidiary, SCCO benefits from scale and an AISC of approximately $0.85/lb — well below the industry average of ~$2.50/lb. This creates substantial free cash flow across copper price cycles. Molybdenum, silver, and zinc are meaningful by-products contributing 15-20% of total revenue. The Tía María project in Peru represents potential 120,000 tonne/year copper addition.

Price Sensitivity

SCCO's extremely low cost structure creates exceptional operating leverage. Each $0.10/lb increase in copper adds approximately $300M to EBITDA. Molybdenum is a significant credit: each $5/lb change in moly price adds ~$200M annually. The company trades at a premium to peers due to reserve quality and lowest-quartile cost position. SCCO typically pays 90%+ of free cash flow as dividends, making it a high-yield copper play in bull markets.
