Company Overview

📈 Copper Futures Price (HG=F) — SCCO's primary revenue driver

Southern Copper is the Western Hemisphere's largest copper producer, operating low-cost mines at Cuajone, Toquepala, and Buenavista del Cobre in Peru and Mexico. A subsidiary of Grupo Mexico, Southern Copper produces approximately 9% of global copper supply with among the world's lowest all-in sustaining costs (AISC) at roughly $0.85/lb. The company holds one of the largest copper reserve bases globally, with a massive expansion pipeline including the Tía María and Los Chancas projects in Peru that could significantly increase production capacity over the coming decade.

Commodity Exposures

Copper Molybdenum Silver

Copper dominates Southern Copper's revenue, accounting for approximately 80% of sales. Molybdenum, a specialty metal used in high-strength steel alloys and petroleum refining catalysts, provides a meaningful by-product credit that further reduces effective copper production costs. Silver and zinc are also produced as by-products. The ultra-low cost structure means SCCO generates substantial free cash flow across nearly all copper price environments, making it one of the most profitable copper miners globally on a per-pound basis.

Price Sensitivity

SCCO exhibits extremely high copper leverage due to its low-cost structure. Because fixed costs are already covered at relatively low copper prices, incremental price increases flow almost entirely to the bottom line. This operating leverage means small copper price moves translate into large EPS impact — typically 1.5-2x the percentage change in copper price. Molybdenum provides a meaningful secondary revenue stream, with each $5/lb change in molybdenum price adding approximately $200M to annual revenue. The company's Peruvian operations face periodic community opposition and permitting risks that can delay expansion projects.
